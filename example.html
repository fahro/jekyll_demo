Aleph

Search a global archive to identify links across stories and datasets

Read More

OCCRP’s Aleph supports investigations by making data securely searchable. We provide tools to identify links across stories and datasets. Over time, we’re building up a long-term memory of leaks, public records and other data unique to investigative journalism.

Aleph ties together companies, people, payments, documents and leaked e-mails and other core components of follow-the-money investigations. The software supports multiple languages and alphabets, optical character recognition and named entity extraction, as well as the ability to cross-reference a list of entities - such as a list of all the politicians in one country against all the other databases and leaks in the corpus.

With over a billion records covering almost every country, much of which is sensitive, we have rigorous access management controls to ensure we follow journalistic ethics.

Aleph: https://aleph.occrp.org/

Documentation: https://docs.alephdata.org/

If you are interested in setting up your own Aleph, please email data[at]occrp.org

VIS

Build maps of complex business or crime networks

Read More

Visual Investigative Scenarios (VIS) is a data visualization platform designed to assist investigative journalists, activists and others in mapping complex business or crime networks.

The tool provides professionally designed, customizable, dynamic html5 visualization templates allowing you to illustrate entities, networks and complex configurations of data. Visualizations can be exported for use in online, print or broadcast media.

Editorial

Ask OCCRP editors about pitching stories, co-publishing and facilitating cross border investigations

Read More

Our editors help journalists across the OCCRP network turn extremely complicated investigations into stories that readers around the world can understand, share, and act upon. We also coordinate large cross-border investigations, bringing together different journalists and member centers and ensuring that project members are equipped with the right technology, data, and security tools. All journalists in the OCCRP network are invited to pitch stories to our core editorial team in Sarajevo, which provides feedback and editorial support both pre- and post-publication. Our daily news team also produces English-language news coverage of organized crime, corruption, and issues facing journalists worldwide, while our engagement section amplifies the work of our member centers.

ID

Request help from researchers to trace people, companies and assets across the world

Read More

ID is an OCCRP service that helps investigative journalists within the network conduct research quickly and effectively. A team of researchers is on hand to track down people, companies, and assets — like ships and planes — anywhere in the world. They are equipped with access to a number of commercial search engines and databases, as well as the experience to make effective use of dozens of publicly available, but little-known, open sources. With the support of a bespoke ticketing system to shorten the lead time from story inception to publication, the research team helps OCCRP members break new ground in investigative journalism.

Got a research request? Submit a ticket id.occrp.org

Data Desk

Get help with all your data acquisition and analysis questions

Read More

OCCRP’s Data Desk helps journalists to make the most of data. The team supports our network to use Aleph and its ecosystem of tools, including:

  • Aleph - search and find connections in structured and unstructured data
  • VIS Desktop - sketch out networks of people, companies and other entities into a visual diagram of actors and connections
  • Memorious - manage all your web scrapers that import data into Aleph

The team can help you import large datasets into Aleph, analyze data to find answers to important questions and scrape data from websites and parse data from documents. If you work for OCCRP or an OCCRP member centre, you can access this service by emailing data[at]occrp.org

Info and Physical Security

Training and advice on best practices, which tools to use and how to stay safe

Read More

We take the security of those we work with seriously. OCCRP runs in-person counter surveillance trainings to better respond to physical security risks.

Benefit from on-site security audits of your center and in some cases we can fund security purchases to enhance the safety of your working environment.

We visit member centres to run bespoke digital security trainings and check-ups, helping to set up secure channels of communication and share best practice. Our technical experts provide emergency support and can help you respond to security incidents.
